üìñ Core Concepts

Vector Definition

A vector has magnitude (length) and direction:

$$ \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(2D)} \quad \text{or} \quad \vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(3D)} $$

Key Operations

Addition

$$ \vec{a} + \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} a_x + b_x \\ a_y + b_y \end{bmatrix} $$

Scalar Multiplication

$$ c \cdot \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} c v_x \\ c v_y \end{bmatrix} $$

üßÆ Dot & Cross Products

Dot Product

$$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_x b_x + a_y b_y $$

Measures vector alignment. Used in:

  • Angle calculations
  • Projections
  • Portfolio covariance

Cross Product (3D)

$$ \vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ a_x & a_y & a_z \\ b_x & b_y & b_z \end{vmatrix} $$

Finds perpendicular vector. Used in:

  • Area calculations
  • Torque computations

üìà Financial Applications

Portfolio as Vectors

Asset weights can be represented as vectors:

$$ \text{Portfolio} = \begin{bmatrix} w_1 & w_2 & \cdots & w_n \end{bmatrix} $$

Covariance Matrix

Asset correlations relate to dot products of normalized return vectors:

$$ \text{Cov}(X,Y) = \frac{\vec{X} \cdot \vec{Y}}{n} - \mu_X \mu_Y $$
