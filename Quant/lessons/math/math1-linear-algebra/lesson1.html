üìñ Definitions & Concepts

A vector is a quantity with magnitude and direction. In coordinates:

$$ \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix}, \quad \vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} $$

Unlike a point, a vector does not represent a position but a displacement.

The sum of two vectors:

$$ \vec{v} + \vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 + u_1 \\ v_2 + u_2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Scalar multiplication:

$$ c \cdot \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} c v_1 \\ c v_2 \end{bmatrix} $$

üßÆ Dot & Cross Product

The dot product of two vectors gives a scalar:

$$ \vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} = \|v\|\|u\|\cos(\theta) $$

You can compute the angle between vectors using arccos:

$$ \theta = \cos^{-1} \left( \frac{\vec{v} \cdot \vec{u}}{\|v\| \|u\|} \right) $$

Cross product exists in 3D and returns a perpendicular vector. Useful for calculating area and normals.
